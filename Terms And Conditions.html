WorkSphere Terms and Conditions

Terms

1. Scope of Work

  • Services Provided: Detail what you will deliver (e.g., design, writing, coding).
  • Deliverables: Specify outputs (e.g., a logo, article, app feature).
  • Exclusions: Clarify what is not included to avoid misunderstandings.

2. Payment Terms

  • Fees: Total cost, hourly rate, or payment structure.
  • Payment Schedule: Upfront deposit (e.g., 30-50%), milestone payments, and final payment terms.
  • Payment Methods: Bank transfer, PayPal, etc.
  • Late Payment Penalty: Include interest or fees for overdue payments (optional).

3. Deadlines and Timelines

  • Project Timeline: Start date, deadlines for deliverables, and project completion date.
  • Delay Management: Specify penalties for missed deadlines and how delays caused by the client will be handled.

4. Revisions Policy

  • Number of free revisions (e.g., "up to 3 revisions").
  • Cost for additional revisions beyond the agreed number.

5. Intellectual Property (IP)

  • Ownership transfer terms (e.g., client owns final deliverables after full payment).
  • Retention rights (e.g., freelancer may showcase work in their portfolio).

6. Confidentiality

  • Mutual agreement not to share sensitive information.
  • Protection of proprietary data provided by the client.

7. Termination Clause

  • Grounds for termination by either party.
  • Compensation terms for completed work if terminated early.

8. Liability and Indemnity

  • Freelancer is not responsible for damages caused by the use of the deliverables.
  • Liability is limited to the total amount paid by the client.

9. Force Majeure

  • Exclude liability for delays caused by events beyond control (e.g., natural disasters, pandemics).

10. Client Responsibilities

  • Assets or information the client must provide.
  • How delays in client responses or approvals will affect deadlines.

11. Dispute Resolution

  • Methods to resolve disputes (e.g., negotiation, mediation, arbitration).
  • Jurisdiction and governing law.

12. Entire Agreement

  • State that the written terms represent the entire agreement, and changes must be in writing.

Conditions

1. Work Scope Conditions

  • Clearly define the scope of work.
  • Ensure that additional tasks or changes beyond the agreed scope are treated as separate projects or chargeable add-ons.
  • Require a written agreement for any scope modifications.

2. Payment Conditions

  • Specify payment terms, such as:
    • Upfront deposit (e.g., 30%-50%) before starting the project.
    • Payment upon milestones or project completion.
  • Indicate acceptable payment methods (e.g., bank transfer, PayPal, Stripe).
  • Include late payment penalties, such as interest or suspension of work.

3. Timeliness and Deadlines

  • Set clear deadlines for deliverables.
  • Include provisions for delays, such as:
    • Extensions if caused by client delays.
    • Penalties for missed deadlines (if applicable).
  • Require timely feedback or approvals from the client to avoid delays.

4. Revision Policy

  • Allow a specific number of revisions (e.g., 2-3) as part of the project fee.
  • Charge for additional revisions beyond the agreed number.
  • Set a time limit for revisions (e.g., "Revisions must be requested within 7 days of delivery").

5. Ownership and Intellectual Property

  • State that the client owns the final deliverables only after full payment is received.
  • Retain ownership of unused concepts, drafts, or raw files unless otherwise agreed.
  • Reserve the right to showcase completed work in your portfolio or marketing materials, unless confidentiality is required.

6. Confidentiality Conditions

  • Agree to keep all sensitive client information confidential.
  • Require the client to respect your work processes and tools.

7. Termination and Cancellation

  • Specify conditions for terminating the contract, such as:
    • Non-payment by the client.
    • Failure to deliver work by the freelancer.
  • State refund policies for cancellations (e.g., "Deposit is non-refundable").
  • Agree on how incomplete work will be handled in case of termination.

8. Liability and Warranty

  • Limit liability to the total amount paid by the client.
  • Disclaim liability for indirect damages or losses caused by the deliverables.
  • Offer a limited warranty period for corrections (if applicable).

9. Communication Conditions

  • Set expectations for response times (e.g., "Replies within 1-2 business days").
  • Specify preferred communication methods (e.g., email, messaging platforms).

10. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

  • State the jurisdiction and laws governing the agreement.
  • Include steps for resolving disputes (e.g., mediation, arbitration).

11. Non-Exclusivity

  • Clarify that the freelancer may work with other clients simultaneously unless an exclusivity agreement is made.

12. Force Majeure

  • Exclude responsibility for delays caused by unforeseen events like natural disasters, illness, or emergencies.
